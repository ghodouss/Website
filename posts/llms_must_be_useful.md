Whenever I discuss the limitations of LLMs, I find myself always having to preface the discussion with an acknowledgement of the use cases in which LLMs are useful or could help people.

Some LLM use cases fit the first bucket — great fit, great taste, but altogether not impactful enough to justify the investment to support the use-case. However, I think most applications of LLMs fall into the second bucket — it technically works today if I don't squint, but it's the wrong tool for the wrong job with soon-to-eventual decay.
